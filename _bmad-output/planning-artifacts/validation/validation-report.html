PRD body is decision-ready; the new calendar FRs aren't yet. The Calendar feature group is unusually strong on its safety thesis (escalate-on-uncertainty, read-only scope, encrypted token storage, default-off) and has no conflicts with the decision log. But the adversarial pass rates it NOT READY for architecture: the crypto prose outruns the product spec.

Four load-bearing questions are unanswered — whose calendar answers (multi-operator), which service a free-text Russian question maps to, whose timezone interprets the customer's time, and recovery when a token is revoked Google-side. Two operational time-bombs the "read-only first" framing hides — Google OAuth app-verification for the sensitive scope, and refresh-token long-term expiry — must be named as release dependencies.
